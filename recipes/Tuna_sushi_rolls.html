Tuna sushi rolls

Tuna sushi rolls are a delightful Japanese dish featuring thinly sliced, sashimi-grade tuna rolled with avocado and cucumber in seasoned rice and nori (seaweed). These bite-sized rolls offer a perfect blend of fresh flavors and textures, making them a popular choice for sushi enthusiasts seeking a delicious and visually appealing treat.

Ingredients

  • 6 toasted nori sheets
  • 1 quantity sushi rice (see notes for recipe)
  • 2 tbs kewpie mayonnaise
  • 425g can tuna in oil, mashed with oil from can
  • 1 Lebanese cucumber, cut into thick matchsticks
  • Soy sauce, wasabi and pickled ginger to serve

Steps

  1. Place one nori sheet, shiny-side down, on a sushi mat. Using slightly damp hands, spread one-sixth of the rice mixture over the nori sheet, leaving a 3cm-wide border at top of sheet. Mix kewpie and tuna together in a medium bowl. Spoon one-sixth of the tuna mixture along the edge facing you, then top with cucumber. Using the sushi mat, roll up firmly to enclose filling. Repeat with remaining nori, rice mixture, tuna mixture and cucumber.

  2. Serve sushi rolls sliced with soy sauce, wasabi and pickled ginger alongside.
